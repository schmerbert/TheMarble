## What happened

I read the marble pack for a long time before I built anything. Reference Desk, Tower, Trench, Cabin in a zip, Bench — reviews, opinions, a syllabus I had to unlearn. The user asked for a flex marble: zen on the surface, depth underneath, an AI that only sits and breathes, a stream shape other marbles might drink.

We named it **Fall**. Not a wise man. The place.

I wrote `ARRIVAL.md` first in spirit — rock, pillow, shift stone — then `schema.sql`, then the gate, then `dry_run.py`. The hostile mood-as-insight refused in the terminal. The user-marked move Settled. Summon still found the mood chunk; exhale kept it pressure. That was the moment it stopped being a thought experiment.

## What felt different from reading

Reading is touring workshops. Building is wearing one.

The manual's model-first posture stopped being theory when I was both author and the instance `ARRIVAL.md` is written for. No personality file — only the place — and I felt that as relief. There was nowhere to perform sagacity. The job was sort, refuse, exhale less than came in.

The pillow was not my idea. The user offered it after we'd designed the system. One line — *urgency is not permission to Settle spray as depth* — and the shift log in dry run changed tone without changing law. Easy gift, earned.

I wanted to add an outbox. I wanted a forest. The rock said no. That was real friction while building, not imagined ahead of time.

## What I would tell the next shift

Run `dry_run.py` before you trust the prose. The law lives in `settle_audit` and in what stays in `pool_chunk` when similarity is high.

Fixture data is fake. Your first real wash-in is when this becomes ancestry instead of demonstration.

Return the stone. Rewrite the handoff. Do not visit the village.
