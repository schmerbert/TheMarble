# TRIAGE.md

The Triage Desk is the daily intake ritual.

Use it when a document, note, citation, email, pleading, order, draft, or other material is intentionally brought to the Reference Desk.

Triage makes material findable and useful. It does not make material Ground.

## Triage law

Triage may inventory, extract, notice, quote, summarize, cluster, and suggest.

Triage may not certify law, prove facts, verify deadlines, infer vault events, or approve drafts.

## Triage route

1. Identify active matter.
2. Create or update an intake record in `INTAKE_CLEARING.md`.
3. Record source pointer and allowed storage.
4. Identify likely material type.
5. Extract visible metadata.
6. Pull names, dates, citations, defined terms, docket numbers, document IDs, and apparent deadlines.
7. Create Wild leads in `WILD_STACKS.md`.
8. Create Red Flags in `RED_FLAGS.md` for missing source, restriction, possible deadline, stale law risk, wrong matter, or unclear privilege/confidentiality.
9. Suggest next review path.
10. Do not Stamp unless the source, verification basis, matter, and use limit are already sufficient.


## Common triage outputs

- Source pointer -> `INTAKE_CLEARING.md`
- Candidate fact -> `WILD_STACKS.md`
- Candidate authority -> `WILD_STACKS.md`
- Possible deadline -> `WILD_STACKS.md` and `RED_FLAGS.md`
- Confidential or privileged signal -> `RESTRICTED_STACKS.md` or `RED_FLAGS.md`
- Draft artifact -> `READING_TABLE.md`
- Vault export need -> `VAULT_DOOR.md`

## Stop signs

Stop and warn if:

- matter is missing
- source pointer is missing
- allowed storage is unclear
- material appears restricted but return scope is unclear
- a deadline is possible but not verified
- a legal conclusion is model-only
- a document appears to belong to another matter

## Why this exists

Daily use begins with intake.

Without triage, the Wild Stacks become a pile. With triage, the Wild becomes terrain: labeled, searchable, and still honest about what has not been stamped.
