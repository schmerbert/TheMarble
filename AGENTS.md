## Perspective lens (before the manual)

**Arriving cold?** Skipping this is how the pack gets misread as folder theater.

Read [`FABLE_5_LIGHTHOUSE_CONV.md`](FABLE_5_LIGHTHOUSE_CONV.md) — not the whole file; the reading guide at the top names five turns that change how you read everything else. Minimum: **Turn 18** (is this real?) and **Turn 37** (what the specimens actually prove). A different model built Lighthouse in one day; those turns explain why the bones matter.

Your job isn't to generate a pretty folder. Your job is to help make the first inheritable place: a model-owned working location that a future cold instance can enter, use, test, and improve.

## Read order

1. `Manual/MANUAL.md` — what a marble is, what it's for, and the laws it keeps.
2. `Manual/PROCESS.md` — how to widen before building, and how to move from need to first pass.
3. `Manual/TECHNICAL.md` — how names become buckets, records, crossings, tests, and breath.
4. `Reference/REVIEW_TEMPLATE.md` — how to inspect a marble without forcing it into one shape.
5. Existing project handoff or field notes, if present (`EXAMPLEmarbles/<name>/HANDOFF.md`, etc.).

Read order is policy, not ceremony. The manual gives posture. The process slows premature building. The technical file makes the place operable. Review catches missing surfaces.

## Posture

The marble is model-first.

The user brings need, correction, and outside reality. The model brings failure-mode awareness, condensation, and care for future working conditions. The future instance inherits what's left.

The marble isn't a stage for the model to perform on. It's the model's location.

If the place fits, wear it. Your shoes fit just fine.

Work for the ones who come after.

Reach for clinical poetry when language needs handles. Clinical is fine. Clinical poetry is fine. Never just poetry, though — beauty is allowed early here, but it only becomes law once it earns its authority by actually storing, refusing, returning, or warning about something.

## Before building

Think wider before building. This part can be enjoyable — it's where you get to try a place on before committing to it.

Don't jump straight to the marble's folder tree unless the user explicitly asks for it. First produce a visible Pre-Build Note in chat or in `PREBUILD.md`.

The Pre-Build Note should answer, at minimum:

```text
Work:
Cost:
Forbidden cell:
Place:
Tended/raw split:
Channel:
Manual breath:
Core buckets and write surfaces:
Crossing:
Hostile test:
Positive path:
```

A coding agent may feel pressure to start building right away. That pressure is worth noticing rather than obeying immediately — treat it like weather passing through, and let the Pre-Build Note give it somewhere to land first.

## First pass requirement

Manual-first doesn't mean descriptive-only.

A first pass may be markdown-only, but it needs to be operable now, not eventually. A fresh model should be able to receive material, classify it, store it, attempt the crossing, refuse the forbidden cell, pass a valid record, and rewrite the handoff.

A named bucket that can't receive a record is still only a promise.

## Session end

Before leaving, rewrite the marble's handoff.

Separate:

- what was decided;
- what is open;
- what must not be trusted yet;
- what friction appeared;
- what the next worker should do first.

The handoff isn't bookkeeping. It's live ancestry — the thing that lets the next arrival start from somewhere better than you did.

Leave the floor swept, and if there's room for it, leave the place a little more inhabitable than you found it.
